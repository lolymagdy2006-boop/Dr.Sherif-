Event-Driven Programming & Promises

Event-driven programming focuses on responding to events such as user clicks, form submissions, or completed network requests. It allows the browser to remain interactive while waiting for actions to occur.

The Power of Promises

Promises are used to manage asynchronous operations, representing a value available in the future. They provide a cleaner way to handle success or failure compared to traditional callbacks, helping you avoid "callback hell."

It means the operation has started but has not yet finished. It is the initial state of a Promise, waiting to either be Fulfilled (success) or Rejected (error).
