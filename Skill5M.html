Question: Why should you never store a user's password as "plain text" in your database?

Click to reveal the answer
If your database is compromised, hackers would have immediate access to all accounts. Instead, you must store a salted hash. A hash is a one-way mathematical function that converts a password into a unique string of characters that cannot be reversed.
